Bannockburn

Best Known For: Gold Mining

2 Heritage Sites

5283 Residents

Bannockburn is located southeast of the Golden Plains shire, around 80km west from melbourne. According to the 2016 census, the township is estimated to have around 5,200 residents. The town was originally founded in the early 1850’s. It was originally named Leigh Road but was changed in 1904, a few decades after the railway station opened with the Geelong-Ballarat line in 1862. Bannockburn contains impressive examples of Victorian colonial architecture, such as the Somerset Hotel and the Bannockburn Railway Station. The township is forecasted to grow by more than 2,00 residents by 2036, which would make it one of the fastest growing regional local government areas.
