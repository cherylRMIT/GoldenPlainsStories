Located 106km west of Melbourne and 18km west of Bannockburn, Shelford is a small town, initially populated by soldier settlers and families after World War 2 and based on the 2016 census now has a population of roughly 250 people - only a 120 increase in population from what it was in the 1930’s. Shelford is currently best known for its Annual Duck Race - a major event that unites the local communities and provides opportunities for friends and families to come together. Shelford's other stand-out sites also include the Shelford Post Office (1847), the Shelford Primary School (1866), the Leigh Shire Hall and Toll House (1871-1872), and the Shelford Bridge built on the main road from Melbourne to Portland (1874).
