Teesdale is a small township in the southeastern region of the shire. The township was apparently named after an outstation of T&S Learmonth who were pastoralists who occupied the Native Creek in the 1830s. Soon after, in 1851, the first District Surveyor described this area as “Thickly wooded principally of She oak”. Although the original trees were cleared for firewood and materials by settlers, the Drooping She-oaks have returned to the area.
