Smythesdale is located in the northwestern region of the shire, mostly located in the Golden Plains Shire but also partially located in the Shire of Pyrenees. The township was named after John Smythe, who took up a pastoral lease in the area in 1849. The township was intially known as Smythe's Creek when it was established during the Victorian Gold Rush, however Smythesdale earned its current name in 1864.
