Agriculture

For thousands of years, the Wadawurrung People studied the landscape and environment around them to provide all the food, clothing and shelter the people needed. The wadawurrung people lived in rich culture and rituals for thousands of years. The indeigenous people used the resources within the land to sustain survival. They used sustainable farming and agriculture to ensure they took more than they needed and always made sure nothing went to waste.

The Story of Bunji
From Dreamtime legend, over 60,000 years ago, Bunjil (a wedge tail eagle) shaped the earth's landscape and created people and animals, and defined laws for the people to live by. He then became a star to live forever.

Colonisation
Wadawurrung People lived in this region for thousands of generations, until 1835 when John Batman signed a treaty with the Wunrundjeri People to sell the land for sheep grazing. The Wadawurrung people call them ‘Squatters’, which was rapidly occupied after this treaty was signed. By 1851 the land had over 5 million sheep, which dramatically changed the landscape and destroyed the traditional food sources of the Wadawurrung People. Additionally, the impact of the gold rush further destroyed the natural environment which was essential for the traditional lifestyle of the indigenous people.
