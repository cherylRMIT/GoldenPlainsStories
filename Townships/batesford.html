Batesford is divided by the Golden Plains shire and the City of Greater Geelong, approximately 70km southwest of melbourne. The town is based around the Mooraboola river. As of the 2016 census, Batesford has a population of 952 residents. The township's main feature is the large granite outcrop, called the “Dog Rocks”. It obtained its name from wild local dogs that used to live around the rocks. In indigenous culture it is believed the ‘Dog Rocks’ are haunted.
