Haddon is a town 12km from Ballarat located in the Golden Plains shire with a population of 1,194 residents from the 2016 census. The area was first known as ‘Sago Hill’ or ‘Campbells Diggins’, as there had been prospect for gold in 1857 but not profited until 1859. By 1870 the town was changed from ‘Sago Hill’ to ‘Haddon', and maintained a population of over 400 people until the early 1880’s. The railway was built and connected to Ballarat in 1883. Towards 1900 gold mining declined.
