Dereel is a town located in the north-west of the Golden Plains shire, 33km from the city of Ballarat. From the 2016 census, Dereel and the surrounding areas have a population of 533 people. The name ‘Dereel’ is thought to be derived from the aboriginal word description of a tribal boundary. The town's settlement was supported by timber cutting, gold mining and grazing. Although there was gold found in the surrounding area of Rokewood in 1855, there was no rush recorded. By 1864 settlement was sufficient for a school to open. Mining in surrounding areas continued past 1900 but by the 1930’s the population fell to around 100 people and the school unfortunately closed. Since 1974 the rural town has attracted new residents and has been able to open up a general store, a nursery, engineering works and a memorial hall.
