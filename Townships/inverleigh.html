Inverleigh is a rural town located 28km from Geelong, however the township is divided between the Golden Plains Shire and the Surf Coast Shire. According to the 2016 census, Inverleigh has a population of 1,474 residents. The town doesn’t have a lot of history recorded but was named after the Leigh river, which was named by the colonial surveyor in 1835. However, there have been recorded conflicts in 1837 and 1838, with a group of indigenous people attacking the lower station of Clyde Company with two people dying and one severely injured.
