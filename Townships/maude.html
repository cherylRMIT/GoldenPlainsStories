Maude, Steiglitz and She Oaks are three distinct towns located on the east side of the Golden Plains shire. From the 2016 census, the towns have a total of 383 residents. The townships have a history of gold mining and farming. The towns were first settled in 1836. Maude has always been a very small village, with an average population of 200 since 1911. Steiglitz is located on the Sutherland creek where gold was discovered in 1854 where the goldfields adapted the town's name. By 1852, Steiglitz grew significantly, which led to a chapel, a courthouse and schools opening up. The most prominent building still remains in the present day, which is the Steiglitz courthouse (which was built in 1875). She Oaks opened a school and a church but was soon moved to Lethbridge after the uniting church was formed.
