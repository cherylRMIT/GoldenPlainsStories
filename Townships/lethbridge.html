The township, Lethbridge is situated outside Geelong in the Golden Plains Shire. Formally named Muddy Water Holes as there were lines of ponds across the Bruces Creek. From the 2016 census, the shire has a population of 1,014 residents. Lethbridge was situated along the horse-carriage tracks that connected Geelong’s port to Ballarat goldfields. This track is still the primary route between the cities but is now the Midland Highway. During the 1800’s the community grew with catholic and government schools opening up. However, in the 1900’s the population declined with a portion of the population were farming by soldiers and part of the Moranghurk estate was subdivided during the Second World War.
